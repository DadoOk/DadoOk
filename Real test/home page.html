Eat in Paris

The Arc de Triomphe de l'Étoile is one of the most famous monuments in Paris. It stands in the centre of the Place Charles de Gaulle at the western end of the Champs-Élysées. It should not be confused with a smaller arch, the Arc de Triomphe du Carrousel, which stands west of the Louvre. The Arc de Triomphe honours those who fought and died for France in the French Revolutionary and the Napoleonic Wars

Ride a camell in Egypt.

Just as important to the pharaohs as their pyramid was a secret chamber called Serdab. A Serdab was close to the pharaoh’s mummy, where his Ka statue (the statue holding the king’s life essence after death) was kept. Ancient Egyptians believed that if the pharaoh’s mummy was destroyed, his spirit could live on inside the Ka statue. Given the very real threat of tombs being robbed and looted the Serdab and Ka statue were hugely important to the pharaoh maintaining a pleasant and peaceful afterlife.
